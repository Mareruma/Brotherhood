House Ironbound is a grim, stoic dynasty of master miners, arcane metallurgists, and golem-smiths who wield absolute economic and military dominance over the mountain megapolis of Xanbiahwortg. Operating from their high forge-courts, they control the vertical logistics of the 13 surface peaks and 40 subterranean layers, standing as the supreme monarchs of global heavy industry and heavy weaponry.

## Wealth beyond Monarchs

The sheer structural power of House Ironbound is a terrifying force of pure metal and coin. Because they extract ten times more material than the local region could ever consume, their 1,000% production surplus has turned the mountain dynasty into an international economic superpower.

* **The Iron Tide:** While foreign kings hoard soft gold coins, House Ironbound counts their wealth in heavy industry. Every time a 10,000-ton Abyss Cruiser from [[House Brightwater]] arrives at the Abyssal Terminal to trade lowland grain, the transaction is balanced with massive, high-security crates of refined steel, gold bars, and golem chassis bound for the Rhaxdanpoint shipping docks.
* **The Iron Phalanx:** The family maintains absolute military authority through the Iron Phalanx—a private army renowned for its devastating black-powder rune-artillery and juggernaut shock-troopers clad in automated golem-plate. Their only loyalty is to the Ironbound bloodline.
* **The Triumvirate Deadlock:** This vast overseas wealth allows the Ironbound lords to engage in a silent, permanent cold war with the lowlands. While House Brightwater tries to leverage the food supply to force lower metal costs, House Ironbound counter-strikes by threatening to skyrocket the price of the iron and machinery required to build and repair Brightwater's shipping fleets. 
## Family Hierarchies & Public Faces

To the millions of miners sweating in the claustrophobic tenements of the Deep-Grip Grid, House Ironbound is a severe but necessary symbol of absolute stability. Their heraldry—a black anvil crossed by a silver lightning bolt—is stamped onto every blast furnace, elevator platform, and iron ration tin as a reminder of who keeps the ceiling from collapsing on their heads.

Behind the stone-carved, heavily guarded gates of the Forge-Courts, however, the family is ruled by military pragmatism, paranoia, and a rigid caste-like structure where usefulness is the only measure of a child's worth.

### Garrick "The Anvil" Ironbound (The Patriarch)
Garrick is a broad, granite-faced patriarch whose skin looks like it was tanned in a blast furnace. To the outside world, he is a stoic, tireless industrial commander who works alongside his foremen in the Sky-Pits. 

In truth, Garrick is an authoritarian tyrant whose soul has become as cold and hard as the iron he refines. He views his family and his miners strictly as industrial inventory. Garrick secretly suffers from an obsessive fear of sabotage, employing a network of faceless spies to monitor the lower tenement taverns. He has been known to seal entire mining shafts—trapping hundreds of innocent workers inside—if he suspects even a single saboteur from a rival lowland house is hidden among them.

### Torin Ironbound (The Eldest Son & Chief Architect)
Torin is the perfect instrument of his father's will. Tall, broad-shouldered, and permanently scarred by foundry sparks, he is the official master of the Sky-Pits and surface smelters. He is a mechanical genius who designed the massive deep-shaft funiculars and automated steam-golems that patrol the city. Torin is calculating, intensely focused on quotas, and harbors a deep, unspoken hatred for [[House Brightwater]], constantly engineering ways to make Xanbiahwortg less dependent on lowland grain.

### Helga Ironbound (The Daughter & Deep Marshal)
Helga is the brutal military fist of the family. Rejecting the politics of the upper courts, she spends her life deep in the dark, dangerous tunnels of the Vault-Roots (Layers 31 to 40). As the active marshal of the Iron Phalanx, she commands the defense lines against Underdark incursions and rogue elementals. Helga is highly perceptive and fiercely loyal to her miners; she has recently begun quietly questioning her father's brutal rationing policies, growing furious that the lower tiers bleed in the dark while the upper courts hoard platinum.

### Brann Ironbound (The Youngest Son & Treasury Envoy)
Brann is the diplomatic and silver-tongued face of the mountain dynasty. Highly educated in foreign law and currency vectors, he spends his time traveling to the coastal docks of [[Rhaxdanpoint]], managing the high-stakes contracts for the 200% overseas metal exports. While he wears the heavy ceremonial plate of his house during diplomatic galas, Brann despises the physical soot and sulfur of the mines. He quietly dreams of using his connections to invest in foreign maritime fleets, aiming to establish an independent Ironbound shipping line that bypasses the lowland monopolies entirely.
