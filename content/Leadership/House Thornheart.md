House Thornheart is the ancient, iron-willed royal bloodline of high-elven and human lineage that holds absolute military and economic sovereignty over the far northeastern megapolis of Drupsall and the untamed northern wilderness. They are the supreme architects of global forestry, the exclusive masters of military-grade ironwood, and the sole gatekeepers of the world's raw building infrastructure.

## Wealth beyond Monarchs

The sheer economic and structural power of House Thornheart eclipses even the wealthiest lowland merchant houses. Because they control the sauszemes (overland) and river flow of every major timber shipment, they effectively hold a financial death-grip over the global transport and mining industries.

* **The Amber Tide:** While other kings measure their wealth in simple grain piles, House Thornheart measures theirs in sovereign timber. Every time a 10,000-ton Abyss Cruiser from [[House Brightwater]] or an industrial convoy from [[House Ironbound]] docks at the Log-Run Delta to trade, a massive percentage of that transaction is instantly converted into rare alchemical resins, pure platinum, and strategic military concessions.
* **The Order of the Silver Blade:** The royal family does not rely on common conscripts or city watchmen. Their absolute authority is enforced by the Order of the Silver Blade—an elite, deeply fanatical knightly order whose weapons and armor are forged from an alchemical fusing of pristine silver and compressed ironwood. They deal devastating damage to woodland monstrosities and rebellious workers alike.
* **The Triumvirate Leverage:** This immense maritime wealth allows King Aldus to stand as the apex of the global economic triangle. House Thornheart uses their timber monopoly as a weapon of political dread: if House Brightwater raises grain prices, the Winter King threatens to completely cut off the lumber required to repair the *Abyss Cruisers* and trade ships, forcing the lowlanders to their knees.

## Family Hierarchies & Public Faces

To the working classes of the Briar-Tenements and the isolated logging camps along the Wild-Front, House Thornheart is a shining symbol of regal majesty, ancient lineage, and unyielding defense against the terrors of the deep woods. Their heraldry—a silver blade piercing a frozen wooden crown—is carved onto every checkpoint and fortress gate as a promise of absolute order.

Behind the majestic white stone walls of the High Canopy, however, the royal palace is a severe, militaristic court where empathy is treated as a sin, and weakness is punished by exile or execution into the predator-filled wilds.

### King Aldus Thornheart (The Winter King)
King Aldus is a towering, scar-faced monarch who rules with absolute military discipline and serves as the Supreme Grand Master of the Order of the Silver Blade. Having personally spent his youth bleeding to conquer the northern wild-front from ancient beasts, he has been completely hardened by war. 

In truth, Aldus is a monarch driven by a cold, calculating paranoia. He views his subjects not as citizens, but as expendable cogs in a global industrial machine. He routinely signs execution orders for starving workers who fail to meet logging quotas, covering up the resulting unrest through brutal military crackdowns. He views his children strictly as assets to secure his sovereign line.

### Crown Princess Serephina (The Eldest Daughter & General)
Serephina is the perfect extension of her father's iron will. Serving as the active High Marshal of the Silver Blade, she is an impeccably trained tactical genius who personally commands the brutal clear-cutting operations along the Wild-Front. Serephina has no patience for diplomacy or coin-flipping politics; she believes the forest is an active enemy that must be methodically slaughtered. She commands absolute loyalty from the knights and views her younger brother’s diplomatic games with deep, unspoken contempt.

### Prince Kaelen (The Youngest Son & Envoy)
Kaelen is the charming, highly educated public face of the royal court. As the kingdom's chief envoy, he spends his life navigating the high-stakes contracts for the 200% overseas timber exports, coordinating directly with the embassies in Rhaxdanpoint. Kaelen abhors physical combat and the bloody reality of the Wild-Front logging camps. He is deeply fascinated by the spatial mechanics of House Brightwater’s dimensional wagons, quietly scheming to adapt that magic to compress whole forests into a single cargo container to bypass his sister's traditional shipping routes.
