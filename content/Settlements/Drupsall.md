**Drupsall** is a monumental, fortress-style megapolis situated in the far northeastern reaches of the world. Carved into the sheer northern cliffs and bordered by an untamed, ancient primal forest, it stands as the empire's ultimate military bastion and the global capital of lumber production. The city is a masterpiece of martial architecture—a sprawling grid of ironwood-reinforced stone towers, massive water-powered sawmills, and heavily fortified gates. Drupsall exists for a single purpose: to contain the dangerous northern wilderness while processing millions of tons of high-grade timber to fuel the world's navies and subterranean industries.

## The Districts of Drupsall

Drupsall is built in a series of ascending, fortified tiers that scale the northern cliffs, separating the raw industrial labor of the river valleys from the regal military command centers above:

### 1. The High Canopy (The Royal Court & Knightly Citadel)
Perched upon the highest natural stone cliffs, **The High Canopy** is the crown of Drupsall. It houses the Royal Palace of [[House Thornheart]] and the Grand Cathedral-Fortress of the [[Order of the Silver Blade]].
* **The Living Stone:** The architecture here is a beautiful but severe fusion of white mountain stone and living, magically shaped ironwood trees that form natural arches and defensive parapets.
* **The Gryphon Rookeries:** The highest towers serve as nesting grounds and launch pads for the Order's legendary Gryphon cavalry. The air above the High Canopy is constantly filled with the screech of giant hunting birds patrolling the northern skies.

### 2. The Log-Run Delta (The Industrial Heart & Cruiser Slúžas)
Carved directly around the massive river mouth where the mountain waters cascade down into the lowlands. This is the smoking, buzzing engine of Drupsall.
* **The Sawmill Arrays:** Colossal, water-powered sawmills and arcane cutting wheels operate 24/7, splitting massive primal logs into standardized military planks and ship-masts. The river is choked with a perpetual traffic of floating timber floated down from the outer woods.
* **The Shipping Docks:** This district contains massive, iron-reinforced dry-dock ramps specifically built to receive [[House Brightwater]]’s 10,000-ton *Abyss Cruisers*. The Cruisers arrive packed with lowland grain to feed the city and leave loaded to maximum capacity with treated ironwood, resins, and tar.

### 3. The Briar-Tenements (The Worker Grid)
A dense, multi-layered labyrinth of slate-roofed wood and stone tenements packed tightly into the valleys beneath the sawmills. 
* **The Paramilitary Life:** Hearthway is to Rhaxdanpoint what the Briar-Tenements are to Drupsall. It houses hundreds of thousands of lumberjacks, carpenters, siege engineers, and alchemists. The streets are arranged like military barracks, organized by "Logging Brigades."
* **The Atmosphere:** The air permanently smells of damp sawdust, thick pine resin, and burning peat. Life here is physically brutal; food rations arriving from the lowlands are strictly distributed based on a worker's weekly lumber harvesting quotas, under the watchful eyes of Silver Blade sentries.

### 4. The Wild-Front (The Logging Veil)
The ever-shifting, heavily fortified outer boundary of Drupsall that cuts directly into the primal northern wilderness. It is a terrifying line of logging camps, massive log palisades, and defensive watchtowers.
* **The Combat Zone:** This is where the dangerous felling of ancient ironwood happens. Teams of lumberjacks work in tightly coordinated units under the direct, heavy shield protection of Silver Blade knights.
* **The Unbroken Siege:** The Wild-Front is under constant threat. Primal monsters, aggressive fey creatures, and radical druidic insurgencies launch nightly attacks to smash the sawmills and reclaim the forest, making this district a perpetual battlefield.
## City Mechanics & Local Dynamics

### The White & Silver Peace
Unlike the greedy, corporate atmosphere of Rhaxdanpoint or the smog-choked, brutalist tier-system of Xanbiahwortg, Drupsall feels like an army on a permanent campaign. Laws are absolute, and justice is delivered swiftly by the edge of a Silver Blade knight's sword. Crime in the Briar-Tenements is practically nonexistent, not out of loyalty, but out of fear of the Order’s flawless, militaristic surveillance.

### The Great Wood-Squeeze
Because the city is surrounded by mountains and hostile wildlands, it produces absolutely no grain or meat of its own. Every citizen in the Briar-Tenements knows that their next meal depends on the *Abyss Cruisers* climbing the mountain passes. This makes the city intensely reliant on the lowland logistics, creating a hidden, volatile political tension where the workers despise the lowland merchants but must work themselves to the bone felling ironwood to pay for their daily bread.
