**Xanbiahwortg** is a colossal, subterranean and open-pit mountain megapolis carved directly into the jagged peaks and deep roots of the western mountain range. It is the largest metropolis in the known world, eclipsing even [[Rhaxdanpoint]] in physical scale and raw population. Xanbiahwortg is not interested in trade or luxury; it is a brutalist industrial titan that claws, blasts, and smelts everything that can be extracted from the earth. Every ounce of iron, gold, platinum, and antimagic ore that fuels the world's industries originates from the deep shafts of this mountain citadel.

> [!statblock] Xanbiahwortg Infrastructure & Assets
> - **Type:** Subterranean Megapolis / Industrial Crucible
> - **Primary Output:** Metals, Gemstones, Arcane Ores, and Blacksmithing
> - **Total Population:** Dwarves, Humans, and Underdark Sub-races (Larger than Rhaxdanpoint)
> - **Military Might:** The Iron Phalanx (Heavy Golem-armor infantry and siege engineers)
> - **The Food Deficit:** Consumes ~30,000,000 tons of food annually, entirely dependent on House Brightwater's arrivals.
## The Vertical Districts of Xanbiahwortg

Due to its mountainous geography, Xanbiahwortg is not built horizontally, but vertically and deeply. The city is split into four massive operational tiers:

### 1. The Sky-Pits (The Surface Smelters)
The only part of Xanbiahwortg completely visible to the outside world. The Sky-Pits are vast, tiered open-mine craters and massive stone towers rising above the mountain peaks. 
* **The Pillars of Smoke:** Thousands of industrial blast-furnaces burn day and night, powered by geothermal heat and volcanic gas. The sky above Xanbiahwortg is a perpetual sea of dark soot, ash, and glowing embers, blocking out the sun for miles around.
* **The Foundry Gates:** This tier houses the grand blacksmithing guilds, weapon foundries, and the colossal mechanical lifts that pull raw ore up from the dark depths.

### 2. The Abyssal Terminal (The Intake Gate)
Carved into a massive, hollowed-out plateau on the lower eastern slopes of the mountain lies the **Abyssal Terminal**. This is where the mountain meets the outside world's logistics.
* **The Cruiser Station:** This terminal is specifically engineered to handle the massive 10,000-ton *Abyss Cruisers* operated by [[House Brightwater]]. 
* **The Great Exchange:** Because Xanbiahwortg produces no food of its own, the survival of its millions of miners depends entirely on the incoming grain caravans. While Vētras milži (Storm Giants) drag the Cruisers up the steep mountain maģistrāles, the city's gates are lined with massive stone hoppers ready to receive millions of tons of agricultural lifeblood. In return, the Cruisers are re-loaded with heavy crates of refined steel, gold bricks, and maģiskie priekšmeti (magic items) for the lowlands.

### 3. The Deep-Grip Grid (The Living Tenements)
Beneath the foundries lies the true living heart of the megapolis – a sprawling, claustrophobic grid of stone-carved tenements, massive tavern caverns, and fungal farms.
* **The Under-City:** Millions of laborers, miners, engineers, and outcasts live in these artificial caverns. The air is thick with the smell of sulfur, grease, and cheap ale. 
* **The Social Divide:** Unlike Rhaxdanpoint's breezy docks, life in Xanbiahwortg is cold, dark, and damp. The workers are strictly managed by the iron-fisted Mining Overseers, and missing a shift can mean starvation, as food is rationed based on daily mining quotas.

### 4. The Vault-Roots (The Core Shafts)
The lowest and most dangerous territory of Xanbiahwortg, stretching miles into the Underdark. 
* **The Unbroken Dark:** This is where the active mining happens. Colossal steam-powered drills and magical excavation runes shatter the bedrock to extract veins of pristine gemstones, mithril, and mysterious void-ores.
* **The Deep Threat:** The Vault-Roots are constantly expanding, which frequently breaks open hidden, ancient caverns filled with subterranean monsters, rogue elementals, and volatile magic pockets.
## City Mechanics & The Food Leverage

### The Great Dependency
Despite its absolute military might and infinite gold reserves, Xanbiahwortg has one fatal weakness: **it cannot feed itself**. The rocky peaks and dark caverns grow nothing but toxic fungi. If [[House Brightwater]] were to stop the flow of the Abyss Cruisers for even three weeks, the largest megapolis in the world would collapse into a bloody civil war driven by pure starvation. Because of this, the mountain lords treat the Brightwater captains like visiting royalty, despite loathing the family's opportunistic 200% overseas markups.

### The Iron Phalanx
Xanbiahwortg is protected by the **Iron Phalanx**, a military force renowned for its heavy golem-assisted armor and devastating black-powder or rune-siege weaponry. They do not patrol for petty crimes in the tenements; their only job is to protect the foundries, secure the Vault-Roots from Underdark incursions, and guard the Abyssal Terminal where the food arrives.
